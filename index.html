Reducing Dental Anxiety Through AI-Supported Cognitive Models, Personalized Digital Avatars and Biofeedback in Pediatric Dentistry

Problem & Clinical Gap

Pediatric dental anxiety is a multifactorial clinical challenge influenced by emotional regulation, communication, and prior experiences.

Current approaches largely rely on static anxiety assessment methods conducted before or after treatment, offering limited support during the clinical procedure itself.

This gap restricts real-time awareness, negatively affects patient cooperation, and disrupts clinical workflow continuity.

Core Approach: Human-Centered AI

This project introduces a human-centered, AI-assisted framework designed to support — not replace — clinical decision-making.

Artificial intelligence is positioned as an adaptive and ethical support layer rather than a diagnostic authority.

Clinical responsibility and authority remain exclusively with the dentist, ensuring professional autonomy while enhancing situational awareness and patient communication.

How It Works

  • A brief pre-examination digital preparation supports emotional readiness before treatment.
  • Pediatric patients interact with a personalized, age-adaptive digital avatar.
  • Emotional tendencies and communication needs are dynamically recognized.
  • Minimalist, narrative-based guidance is provided during the clinical procedure.
  • Optional non-invasive biofeedback contributes to contextual awareness without generating diagnostic decisions.

Impact & Clinical Value

The proposed framework aims to reduce pediatric dental anxiety and improve patient cooperation during clinical procedures.

Expected outcomes include enhanced clinician preparedness, smoother workflow continuity, reduced uncertainty, and lower perceived cognitive load.

Overall, the system offers a scalable, clinically feasible, and ethically safe approach compatible with routine pediatric dental practice.
